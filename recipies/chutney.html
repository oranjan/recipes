Ingredients

  • 1/4 Cup bhang seeds (hemp seeds)
  • 2-3 fresh hot red or green chillies, I used both (or use dried ones or red chilli powder)
  • coriander and mint leaves, chopped (1/2 C or to taste)
  • juice of half a lime (or a souring agent of your choice)
  • salt

Instructions

  1. In a pan, dry roast the bhang seeds on low heat until they become aromatic and slightly browned. Remove from heat and let them cool.
  2. Once the roasted bhang seeds have cooled, grind them into a fine powder using a mortar and pestle or a spice grinder.
  3. Once the roasted bhang seeds have cooled, grind them into a fine powder using a mortar and pestle or a spice grinder.
  4. Blend the ingredients together until you get a coarse paste. You can add a little water if needed to facilitate the blending process.
  5. Once the chutney has reached the desired consistency, transfer it to a bowl.
  6. Add lemon juice and salt to taste, and mix well.
  7. Taste the chutney and adjust the seasoning or spice level according to your preference.
  8. Allow the flavors to meld together by refrigerating the chutney for at least 30 minutes before serving.
  9. Serve Kumaoni Bhang ki Chutney as a condiment or accompaniment to snacks, parathas, or traditional Kumaoni dishes.
