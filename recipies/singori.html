Singori ki Mithai

Singori - A dessert from the hills

Simplicity at its best!! I again dedicate this to the people, who are staying far off, to make them feel connected with their native.This sweet, is a very traditional recipe of Kumaon hills in Uttarakhand, India.


Ingredients

  • 5 servings
  • 250 grams Mava/khoya
  • 100 grams Sugar
  • 100 grams GRATED Coconut dried
  • 1 tsp Cardomom powder
  • 6-7 Almond flakes

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (180°C) or heat a non-stick pan on the stovetop.
  2. In a mixing bowl, combine the grated coconut, khoya, grated jaggery, and cardamom powder. Mix well until all the ingredients are thoroughly combined.
  3. If you are using malta leaves, wash and pat them dry. If using banana leaves, cut them into square-shaped pieces and lightly heat them to make them flexible.
  4. Grease the leaves with cooking oil or ghee to prevent sticking.
  5. Take a small portion of the coconut-khoya mixture and place it in the center of the leaf. Fold the leaf carefully to form a small packet, securing it with a toothpick or kitchen twine.
  6. Repeat the process until you have used all the mixture. Place the Singoris on a baking tray if using an oven or directly on the heated non-stick pan.
  7. If using an oven, bake the Singoris for about 15-20 minutes or until they turn golden brown. If using a stovetop, cook the Singoris on low heat, flipping them occasionally, until they are cooked and golden brown on both sides.
  8. Once cooked, remove the Singoris from the oven or pan and let them cool slightly. Carefully unwrap the Singoris from the leaves.
